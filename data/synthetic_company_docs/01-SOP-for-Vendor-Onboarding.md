## 1. Purpose and Scope

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) establishes the framework and requirements for onboarding new vendors into the procurement system of Northwind Retail Solutions Ltd. ("the Company"). The purpose of this procedure is to ensure that all vendors are properly vetted, approved, and integrated into the Company's supply chain in a consistent, compliant, and risk-managed manner.

This SOP applies to all departments and personnel involved in the vendor selection, evaluation, onboarding, and ongoing management processes. It covers all vendor categories including merchandise suppliers, service providers, technology vendors, logistics partners, and professional consultants engaged by the Company.

The procedure aligns with the Company's Quality Management System requirements, regulatory compliance obligations, and industry best practices for supply chain risk management.

## 2. Definitions and Terminology

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Vendor** | Any external party providing goods, services, or works to the Company |
| **Onboarding** | The structured process of integrating a new vendor into the Company's systems and processes |
| **Procurement Business Partner** | The designated personnel responsible for managing vendor relationships within a business unit |
| **Vendor Risk Assessment** | An evaluation of the potential risks associated with engaging a specific vendor |
| **Master Vendor Agreement** | The formal contract governing the commercial relationship between the Company and a vendor |
| **Approved Vendor List (AVL)** | The official registry of vendors authorized to provide goods or services to the Company |
| **Due Diligence** | The investigative process undertaken to verify vendor qualifications and mitigate risks |

## 3. Responsibilities

### 3.1 Procurement Department

The Procurement Department holds primary ownership of this procedure and is responsible for:

- Maintaining and updating this SOP in accordance with regulatory changes and business requirements
- Establishing and publishing the Approved Vendor List
- Conducting strategic sourcing activities and vendor market analysis
- Negotiating master vendor agreements and commercial terms
- Providing guidance and support to business units on vendor management matters
- Reporting vendor performance metrics to executive leadership

### 3.2 Procurement Business Partners

Procurement Business Partners (PBPs) are assigned to each major business unit and hold the following responsibilities:

- Acting as the primary liaison between the Company and vendors within their portfolio
- Initiating vendor onboarding requests through the formal requisition process
- Coordinating with the Vendor Management Office for risk assessments and compliance reviews
- Monitoring vendor performance against established Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)
- Escalating vendor-related issues to the Procurement Director when appropriate

### 3.3 Vendor Management Office

The Vendor Management Office (VMO) is responsible for:

- Conducting vendor risk assessments and compliance due diligence
- Maintaining vendor records and documentation in the Vendor Information System
- Performing periodic reviews of existing vendors for continued eligibility
- Coordinating with Legal and Compliance teams on contract review and regulatory matters
- Issuing vendor approval or rejection decisions

### 3.4 Information Security

The Information Security team is responsible for:

- Assessing vendors with access to Company data or technology systems
- Conducting cybersecurity due diligence reviews
- Evaluating vendor security certifications and compliance frameworks
- Approving vendor access to Company networks, systems, or sensitive data

### 3.5 Finance and Accounts Payable

Finance and Accounts Payable responsibilities include:

- Verifying vendor tax identification numbers and banking information
- Setting up vendors in the financial system upon approved onboarding
- Processing payments according to agreed commercial terms
- Reconciling vendor statements and resolving discrepancies

### 3.6 Requesting Business Unit

The requesting business unit is responsible for:

- Identifying the business need for a new vendor engagement
- Providing detailed requirements and specifications for requested goods or services
- Participating in vendor evaluation and selection processes
- Validating that delivered goods or services meet requirements

## 4. Vendor Onboarding Process

### 4.1 Process Overview

The vendor onboarding process consists of six primary phases:

1. **Initiation and Requirements Definition**
2. **Vendor Identification and Preliminary Evaluation**
3. **Risk Assessment and Due Diligence**
4. **Commercial Negotiation and Contracting**
5. **System Setup and Activation**
6. **Post-Onboarding Monitoring**

The typical end-to-end timeline for completing vendor onboarding ranges from fifteen (15) to forty-five (45) business days, depending on vendor complexity, risk profile, and contract value.

### 4.2 Phase 1: Initiation and Requirements Definition

#### 4.2.1 Business Need Identification

The process commences when a business unit identifies a requirement for external goods or services. The Procurement Business Partner must be engaged at this stage to ensure adherence to procurement policies.

#### 4.2.2 Purchase Requisition Creation

The requesting manager creates a Purchase Requisition in the Company's Enterprise Resource Planning (ERP) system, providing the following information:

- Detailed description of required goods or services
- Estimated monetary value and budget code
- Required delivery timeline or service commencement date
- Technical specifications or service level requirements
- Preferred vendor suggestions (if applicable)

#### 4.2.3 Requisition Approval

Purchase Requisitions require approval based on defined approval thresholds:

| Requisition Value | Approving Authority |
|-------------------|---------------------|
| Up to $10,000 | Procurement Business Partner |
| $10,001 to $50,000 | Procurement Director |
| $50,001 to $250,000 | Vice President of Business Unit |
| Over $250,000 | Chief Financial Officer + CEO |

### 4.3 Phase 2: Vendor Identification and Preliminary Evaluation

#### 4.3.1 Market Research

The Procurement Business Partner conducts market research to identify potential vendors. Sources include:

- Industry directories and trade publications
- Vendor capability questionnaires
- Peer company recommendations
- Trade show and conference attendance
- Internet-based supplier discovery platforms

#### 4.3.2 Preliminary Vendor Screening

A preliminary screening questionnaire is issued to potential vendors to gather:

- Company background and ownership structure
- Financial stability indicators (e.g., audited financial statements, bank references)
- Relevant certifications (ISO 9001, SOC 2, etc.)
- Experience with similar clients or industries
- Insurance coverage verification

#### 4.3.3 Vendor Shortlisting

Based on questionnaire responses and available market intelligence, a shortlist of two to five qualified vendors is developed for detailed evaluation.

### 4.4 Phase 3: Risk Assessment and Due Diligence

#### 4.4.1 Risk Categorization

All vendors are assigned a risk category upon initial assessment:

| Risk Category | Criteria | Due Diligence Level |
|---------------|----------|---------------------|
| **Low Risk** | Goods/services under $25,000; no data access; standard commercial terms | Basic verification only |
| **Medium Risk** | $25,001 to $250,000; limited data access; custom requirements | Standard due diligence |
| **High Risk** | Over $250,000; significant data access; regulatory impact; single source | Enhanced due diligence |
| **Critical Risk** | Strategic suppliers; critical infrastructure; sensitive data processing | Comprehensive due diligence with executive review |

#### 4.4.2 Due Diligence Requirements

**Standard Due Diligence includes:**

- Verification of business registration and legal standing
- Tax identification number and regulatory compliance confirmation
- Anti-corruption and anti-bribery screening (OFAC, FCPA, UK Bribery Act)
- Financial stability assessment
- Reference verification (minimum two client references)
- Insurance certificate verification

**Enhanced Due Diligence adds:**

- On-site facility visits or virtual assessments
- Detailed financial analysis and credit checks
- Key personnel background verification
- Cybersecurity assessment (for technology vendors)
- Sustainability and ethical sourcing verification

#### 4.4.3 Compliance Review

The Vendor Management Office coordinates with Legal and Compliance to verify:

- Vendor is not on prohibited party lists
- No outstanding legal actions or regulatory sanctions
- Export control and trade compliance (if applicable)
- Data protection and privacy compliance (GDPR, CCPA where applicable)

### 4.5 Phase 4: Commercial Negotiation and Contracting

#### 4.5.1 Request for Proposal

For engagements exceeding $50,000, a formal Request for Proposal (RFP) is issued to shortlisted vendors. The RFP includes:

- Detailed scope of work or product specifications
- Submission requirements and evaluation criteria
- Commercial terms and conditions template
- Timeline for proposal submission and vendor selection
- Presentation or demonstration requirements (if applicable)

#### 4.5.2 Vendor Evaluation

Proposals are evaluated by a cross-functional evaluation committee using a weighted scoring model:

| Evaluation Criterion | Weight |
|---------------------|--------|
| Technical Capability | 35% |
| Commercial Terms | 30% |
| Vendor Stability and Track Record | 20% |
| Risk Mitigation | 10% |
| Sustainability and CSR | 5% |

#### 4.5.3 Contract Negotiation

The Procurement Director or designated negotiator conducts contract negotiations with the selected vendor. Key contract elements include:

- Scope of goods or services
- Pricing structure and payment terms
- Delivery schedules and service levels
- Warranties and representations
- Limitation of liability provisions
- Indemnification clauses
- Termination rights and procedures
- Data protection and confidentiality obligations
- Insurance requirements

#### 4.5.4 Contract Approval

All vendor contracts require approval based on total contract value:

| Contract Value | Approval Authority |
|----------------|---------------------|
| Up to $100,000 | Procurement Director |
| $100,001 to $500,000 | General Counsel + CFO |
| $500,001 to $1,000,000 | CEO |
| Over $1,000,000 | Board of Directors |

### 4.6 Phase 5: System Setup and Activation

#### 4.6.1 Vendor Record Creation

Upon contract execution, the Vendor Management Office creates a vendor record in the Vendor Information System containing:

- Legal entity name and registered address
- Tax identification numbers
- Banking information for payment processing
- Contact information for vendor representatives
- Contract reference and expiration date
- Risk category and due diligence file reference
- Insurance policy details

#### 4.6.2 Financial System Setup

Finance and Accounts Payable establishes the vendor in the financial system, including:

- Vendor code assignment
- Payment terms configuration
- Banking detail validation
- Tax coding setup

#### 4.6.3 Access Provisioning

For vendors requiring access to Company systems:

- Information Security reviews and approves access requests
- System access is provisioned according to principle of least privilege
- Access credentials are issued through formal provisioning workflows
- Access logs are enabled for ongoing monitoring

#### 4.6.4 Approved Vendor List Enrollment

Upon successful completion of all onboarding steps, the vendor is added to the Approved Vendor List with the effective date and contract reference.

### 4.7 Phase 6: Post-Onboarding Monitoring

#### 4.7.1 Initial Performance Review

At ninety (90) days following first delivery or service commencement, the Procurement Business Partner conducts an initial performance review assessing:

- Adherence to delivery schedules
- Quality of delivered goods or services
- Communication and responsiveness
- Compliance with contractual terms

#### 4.7.2 Ongoing Performance Management

Quarterly business reviews are conducted with vendors exceeding $100,000 in annual spend. Reviews include:

- KPI performance against agreed targets
- Contract compliance status
- Risk profile updates
- Continuous improvement opportunities
- Relationship health assessment

#### 4.7.3 Periodic Reassessment

All vendors undergo periodic reassessment based on risk category:

| Risk Category | Reassessment Frequency |
|---------------|------------------------|
| Low Risk | Every 36 months |
| Medium Risk | Every 24 months |
| High Risk | Every 12 months |
| Critical Risk | Every 6 months |

## 5. Controls and Compliance

### 5.1 Internal Controls

The following internal controls are embedded within the vendor onboarding process:

- **Segregation of Duties:** No single individual can initiate and approve vendor onboarding without independent verification
- **Dual Authorization:** Payments above defined thresholds require dual approval
- **System Enforced Workflows:** All onboarding steps are tracked in the ERP system with automated routing and escalation
- **Audit Trail:** All vendor decisions and changes are logged with timestamp and user identification
- **Reconciliation:** Monthly vendor statement reconciliation is performed by Finance

### 5.2 Compliance Requirements

Vendor onboarding must comply with:

- Company Code of Conduct and Ethics Policy
- Anti-Corruption and Anti-Bribery Policy
- Data Protection and Privacy Policy
- Information Security Policy
- Export Control and Trade Compliance Policy
- Environmental, Social, and Governance (ESG) Standards

### 5.3 Record Retention

Vendor documentation and records are retained in accordance with the Company's Records Retention Schedule:

- Vendor contracts: Seven (7) years after termination
- Due diligence files: Seven (7) years after vendor relationship ends
- Financial records: Seven (7) years
- Correspondence: Five (5) years

## 6. Exceptions and Escalations

### 6.1 Emergency Procurement

In circumstances requiring urgent vendor engagement to address operational emergencies, the Procurement Director may authorize expedited onboarding with modified due diligence. All emergency engagements must be documented and regularized within thirty (30) days.

### 6.2 Single Source Justification

When only one vendor is capable of providing required goods or services, a Single Source Justification must be completed and approved by the Chief Financial Officer before proceeding with onboarding.

### 6.3 Rate Card Vendors

Vendors engaged through pre-negotiated corporate rate cards may be onboarded with simplified due diligence, provided the rate card agreement has been properly executed and approved.

## 7. Non-Compliance and Remediation

### 7.1 Unauthorized Vendor Engagement

Engagement of vendors not on the Approved Vendor List without proper authorization is a policy violation subject to disciplinary action. Goods or services received from unauthorized vendors may not be eligible for payment.

### 7.2 Process Deviations

Any deviation from this SOP must be documented and approved by the Procurement Director. Repeated deviations may trigger a process audit and additional controls.

### 7.3 Vendor Performance Failures

Persistent vendor performance failures are addressed through formal corrective action processes. Vendors failing to remediate performance issues within agreed timeframes may be removed from the Approved Vendor List.
