## 1. Purpose and Scope

### 1.1 Purpose

This Policies and Procedures Manual ("Manual") establishes the authoritative framework for governance, risk management, and operational controls at Northwind Retail Solutions Ltd. ("the Company"). The Manual is designed to ensure compliance with applicable laws and regulations, promote ethical business practices, safeguard company assets, and support the achievement of strategic objectives.

### 1.2 Scope

This Manual applies to all employees, officers, directors, contractors, and third-party agents acting on behalf of Northwind Retail Solutions Ltd. The policies herein govern activities conducted at all company locations, remote work environments, and any external business engagements.

### 1.3 Authority

These policies are issued under the authority of the Board of Directors and the Chief Executive Officer. Compliance is mandatory and forms a condition of employment and contractual relationships with the Company.

## 2. Corporate Governance and Oversight

### 2.1 Board of Directors

The Board of Directors comprises seven members, including three independent non-executive directors. The Board meets quarterly and is responsible for:

- Setting the Company's strategic direction
- Approving annual budgets and capital expenditures exceeding $500,000
- Overseeing risk management and internal control systems
- Approving material Related Party Transactions
- Evaluating the performance of the Chief Executive Officer and Senior Leadership Team

### 2.2 Audit Committee

The Audit Committee consists of three independent non-executive directors with relevant financial expertise. The Committee's responsibilities include:

| Responsibility | Frequency | Reporting Line |
|----------------|-----------|----------------|
| External audit oversight | Quarterly | Board of Directors |
| Internal audit review | Bi-monthly | Audit Committee |
| Financial reporting review | Quarterly | Board of Directors |
| Internal control assessment | Semi-annual | Audit Committee |
| Compliance monitoring | Monthly | Chief Compliance Officer |

### 2.3 Delegation of Authority

The Delegation of Authority Matrix defines approval thresholds for business transactions:

- **Operational Expenditure:** Department heads may approve up to $25,000; Directors up to $100,000; C-Suite executives up to $250,000; Board approval required above $250,000
- **Capital Expenditure:** Board approval required for all expenditures exceeding $100,000
- **Contracts:** Legal review required for all contracts exceeding $50,000 or with terms exceeding 12 months
- **Write-offs:** Inventory write-offs exceeding $10,000 require Director-level approval; fixed asset write-offs exceeding $25,000 require CFO approval

## 3. Financial Controls

### 3.1 Segregation of Duties

The Company maintains strict segregation of duties to prevent fraud and errors. No single individual may control all phases of a financial transaction:

- **Authorization:** Approved by department head or designated authority
- **Custody:** Held by designated personnel separate from authorization
- **Recording:** Processed by the Finance Department independent of custody
- **Reconciliation:** Performed by personnel not involved in transaction processing

### 3.2 Cash Management

Cash handling procedures are designed to safeguard company assets:

1. All cash receipts must be deposited within 24 hours of receipt
2. Daily cash counts are required at all retail locations
3. Weekly reconciliation of point-of-sale systems to bank deposits
4. Monthly independent verification of petty cash accounts
5. Quarterly review of bank reconciliations by Finance Department management

### 3.3 Accounts Payable

The Company maintains the following controls over disbursements:

- Three-way matching is required for all vendor invoices (purchase order, receiving report, and invoice)
- Supporting documentation must be attached before payment processing
- Payments are processed on designated cycles (net 30, net 60) per vendor agreements
- All electronic fund transfers require dual authorization for amounts exceeding $10,000
- Pre-authorized payments are reviewed monthly by the Controller

### 3.4 Financial Reporting

Financial statements are prepared in accordance with International Financial Reporting Standards (IFRS). The following timeline governs financial reporting:

| Report | Preparation Deadline | Approval Authority |
|--------|---------------------|-------------------|
| Monthly management accounts | 10th business day | Chief Financial Officer |
| Quarterly financial statements | 20th day post-quarter-end | Audit Committee |
| Annual financial statements | 60 days post-year-end | Board of Directors |
| Statutory filings | As prescribed by law | Company Secretary |

## 4. Operations and Inventory Management

### 4.1 Inventory Control

Inventory represents a significant asset of the Company, and the following controls apply:

**Receiving Procedures:**
- All incoming inventory must be inspected for quality and quantity within 48 hours
- Receiving personnel must be independent of purchasing and accounts payable functions
- Discrepancies between purchase orders and deliveries must be documented and investigated within 5 business days

**Storage Requirements:**
- Inventory must be stored in designated warehouse locations with appropriate climate control
- Hazardous materials must be stored separately and in compliance with applicable regulations
- Physical inventory counts are conducted quarterly at all locations
- Cycle counts are conducted continuously, covering 20% of inventory locations monthly

**Inventory Valuation:**
- Inventory is valued using weighted average cost method
- Lower of cost or net realizable value assessment is performed monthly
- Obsolete inventory reserves are established based on aging analysis and sales projections

### 4.2 Purchase-to-Pay Process

1. **Requisition:** Department initiates purchase requisition in the Enterprise Resource Planning (ERP) system
2. **Approval:** Requisition is routed for approval per Delegation of Authority Matrix
3. **Purchase Order:** Approved requisitions generate Purchase Orders transmitted to vendors
4. **Receiving:** Goods are received and verified against purchase orders
5. **Invoice Processing:** Vendor invoices are matched and processed per accounts payable controls
6. **Payment:** Payments are scheduled per approved payment terms

### 4.3 E-Commerce Operations

The Company's e-commerce platform processes transactions 24 hours a day, 7 days a week. Key controls include:

- Order validation is automated with manual review for orders exceeding $5,000
- Payment card industry (PCI) compliance is maintained through certified payment processors
- Customer refunds require supervisor approval for amounts exceeding $500
- Fraud detection algorithms flag suspicious orders for manual review

## 5. Information Technology and Data Security

### 5.1 Access Controls

The Company employs role-based access control (RBAC) principles:

- System access is granted based on documented job responsibilities
- User access reviews are conducted quarterly by IT Security
- Access rights are revoked within 24 hours of employee termination
- Multi-factor authentication is required for all system access
- Shared accounts are prohibited except where operationally necessary and documented

### 5.2 Data Protection

Personal data and sensitive business information are protected through:

- Data classification: Public, Internal, Confidential, Restricted
- Encryption of data at rest and in transit
- Annual data protection training required for all employees
- Incident response procedures for data breaches
- Data retention schedules per regulatory requirements

### 5.3 Business Continuity

The Company maintains a Business Continuity Plan covering:

- **Recovery Time Objective (RTO):** Critical systems restored within 4 hours
- **Recovery Point Objective (RPO):** Maximum data loss of 1 hour for critical systems
- **Backup procedures:** Daily incremental backups; weekly full backups; monthly off-site storage
- **Disaster recovery testing:** Annual tabletop exercise; bi-annual functional test

### 5.4 Change Management

All changes to production systems must follow the change management process:

1. Change request submitted and documented
2. Technical review and risk assessment
3. Approval from Change Advisory Board
4. Implementation during approved maintenance windows
5. Post-implementation review and verification
6. Documentation updates as required

## 6. Human Resources and Employee Conduct

### 6.1 Code of Conduct

All employees are expected to:

- Act with integrity and in the best interests of the Company
- Avoid conflicts of interest or the appearance thereof
- Maintain confidentiality of proprietary information
- Comply with all applicable laws and company policies
- Report suspected violations through appropriate channels

### 6.2 Conflict of Interest

Employees must disclose any actual or potential conflicts of interest to their direct supervisor and the HR Department. Examples requiring disclosure include:

- Ownership interest in competitors, suppliers, or vendors
- Employment or consulting arrangements with third parties
- Family relationships with individuals employed by competitors or vendors
- Use of company assets, information, or position for personal gain

### 6.3 Whistleblowing

The Company maintains a Whistleblower Policy encouraging employees to report concerns without fear of retaliation. Reports may be made to:

- Direct supervisor
- HR Department
- Internal Audit
- Anonymous hotline (managed by independent third party)

All reports are investigated, and confidentiality is maintained to the extent practicable.

### 6.4 Employee Separation

Upon termination or resignation:

- Access to all company systems is revoked within 24 hours
- Exit interview is conducted
- Company property is returned and documented
- Final paycheck is processed per applicable labor laws
- Non-compete and non-solicitation provisions are communicated

## 7. Compliance and Regulatory Requirements

### 7.1 Regulatory Framework

The Company operates under the following regulatory frameworks:

- Sarbanes-Oxley Act (SOX) compliance for publicly traded subsidiary
- General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) for European customer data
- Payment Card Industry Data Security Standard (PCI DSS)
- Consumer protection laws in applicable jurisdictions
- Tax and customs regulations in all operating countries

### 7.2 Compliance Monitoring

The Compliance function monitors adherence to regulatory requirements through:

- Quarterly compliance self-assessments by department
- Annual independent compliance audits
- Continuous monitoring of regulatory updates
- Training and awareness programs
- Incident tracking and remediation follow-up

### 7.3 Anti-Money Laundering

The Company has zero tolerance for money laundering and terrorist financing. All employees must:

- Verify customer identity per Know Your Customer (KYC) procedures
- Report suspicious transactions through the designated reporting mechanism
- Refrain from tipping off individuals about suspicious activity investigations
- Complete annual anti-money laundering training

## 8. Risk Management Framework

### 8.1 Risk Assessment

The Company employs a structured risk assessment process:

1. **Risk Identification:** Annual comprehensive risk identification exercise
2. **Risk Analysis:** Assessment of likelihood and impact
3. **Risk Evaluation:** Prioritization against risk appetite
4. **Risk Treatment:** Development of mitigation strategies
5. **Monitoring and Review:** Continuous monitoring and periodic reassessment

### 8.2 Risk Categories

| Category | Key Risks | Risk Owner |
|----------|------------|------------|
| Strategic | Market competition, economic conditions, changing consumer preferences | Chief Executive Officer |
| Operational | Supply chain disruption, inventory shrinkage, system failures | Chief Operating Officer |
| Financial | Credit risk, currency exposure, liquidity | Chief Financial Officer |
| Compliance | Regulatory changes, litigation, data breaches | Chief Compliance Officer |
| Technology | Cybersecurity, system obsolescence, data loss | Chief Information Officer |

### 8.3 Risk Appetite

The Board has established the following risk appetite thresholds:

- **Residual risk** must be maintained at or below medium for all operational risks
- **High residual risk** requires immediate escalation to the Audit Committee
- **Risk acceptance** for medium-level risks requires CFO approval
- **Risk acceptance** for high-level risks requires Board approval

## 9. Document Control and Record Retention

### 9.1 Document Hierarchy

The Company maintains the following document hierarchy:

1. **Policies:** Board-approved statements of principle and direction
2. **Procedures:** Detailed step-by-step instructions for executing processes
3. **Guidelines:** Recommended practices that allow for professional judgment
4. **Forms and Templates:** Standardized documents for consistent execution

### 9.2 Record Retention

The following retention periods apply to key business records:

| Record Type | Retention Period | Storage Medium |
|-------------|------------------|----------------|
| Financial statements | 7 years | Electronic |
| Tax returns and supporting documentation | 7 years | Electronic |
| Contracts | 10 years after expiration | Electronic + Physical |
| Employee records | 7 years after separation | Electronic |
| Board and Committee minutes | Permanent | Electronic + Physical |
| Audit reports | 7 years | Electronic |
| Customer transaction records | 7 years | Electronic |

### 9.3 Document Approval

- Policies require approval from the policy owner and relevant executive
- Procedures require approval from the process owner and Quality Assurance
- Changes to controlled documents must follow the document change request process
- Obsolete documents must be removed from circulation and archived

## 10. Policy Review and Amendments

### 10.1 Review Schedule

This Manual is subject to the following review cycle:

- **Annual Review:** Comprehensive review of all policies by policy owners
- **Ad hoc Updates:** Amendments issued as needed in response to regulatory changes, operational changes, or identified deficiencies
- **Board Review:** Full Manual presented to Board annually for re-affirmation

### 10.2 Amendment Process

1. Change request submitted to Document Control Administrator
2. Impact assessment conducted
3. Stakeholder review and comment period (10 business days)
4. Approval by appropriate authority per change type
5. Document version updated
6. Communication of change to affected parties
7. Training updated as required

### 10.3 Communication

All employees are notified of policy changes through:

- Email notification with summary of changes
- Updated document posted to Company intranet
- Training sessions for material changes
- Acknowledgment required for significant policy updates
