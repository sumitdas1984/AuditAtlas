## 1. Executive Summary

This Risk Register provides a comprehensive overview of the key risks facing Northwind Retail Solutions Ltd. as of the first quarter of fiscal year 2026. The register has been prepared in accordance with the company's Enterprise Risk Management (ERM) Policy (POL-COO-007) and aligns with the COSO ERM Framework (2017).

The current risk landscape reflects a period of significant transformation as the company executes its omnichannel growth strategy while navigating macroeconomic headwinds, evolving regulatory requirements, and heightened cybersecurity threats. This document identifies 24 active risks across six categories, with 4 risks rated as Critical, 8 as High, 9 as Medium, and 3 as Low.

The aggregate risk profile has shifted modestly upward since the previous quarter, primarily due to increased supply chain disruption risk and emerging privacy regulations in jurisdictions where the company operates its e-commerce platform. The Board Risk Committee reviewed and approved this register at its meeting on January 12, 2026.

## 2. Risk Assessment Methodology

### 2.1 Risk Rating Scale

Risks are assessed using a 5×5 risk matrix combining Likelihood and Impact scores:

| Rating | Likelihood Score | Impact Score |
|--------|------------------|--------------|
| Critical | 5 | 5 |
| High | 4 | 4 |
| Medium | 3 | 3 |
| Low | 2 | 2 |
| Minimal | 1 | 1 |

**Risk Score = Likelihood × Impact**

### 2.2 Risk Rating Thresholds

| Risk Score Range | Rating | Escalation Requirement |
|------------------|--------|------------------------|
| 20–25 | Critical | Immediate Board notification |
| 15–19 | High | Executive Committee notification within 48 hours |
| 8–14 | Medium | Risk Committee notification within 5 business days |
| 1–7 | Low | Quarterly reporting only |

## 4. Critical Risk Deep Dives

### 4.1 COMP-001: GDPR and Global Privacy Non-Compliance

**Background:** Northwind Retail Solutions Ltd. processes personal data for approximately 4.2 million active customer accounts across 14 countries. The company operates e-commerce platforms subject to GDPR, CCPA, UK GDPR, and emerging privacy laws in Canada, Australia, and several Asia-Pacific jurisdictions.

**Key Controls:**
- Data Protection Officer (DPO) appointed and functioning since March 2024
- Privacy impact assessments required for all new data processing activities
- Data subject access request (DSAR) workflow automated via case management system
- Cross-border data transfer mechanisms (Standard Contractual Clauses, adequacy decisions)
- Annual privacy training mandatory for all employees with system access

**Control Testing Schedule:** Internal Audit plans to perform a comprehensive privacy control testing in Q2 2026, with external advisor verification in Q3 2026.

**Residual Risk:** Following control implementation, residual risk remains High due to the complexity of cross-border data flows and the evolving regulatory landscape.

### 4.2 TECH-001: Cybersecurity Breach

**Background:** The retail sector remains a primary target for threat actors. Northwind Retail Solutions Ltd. experienced 847,000 intrusion attempts and 12 confirmed phishing incidents in 2025. No material data exfiltration occurred, but the increasing sophistication of attacks requires ongoing vigilance.

**Key Controls:**
- 24/7 Security Operations Center (in-house with managed detection and response partner)
- Multi-factor authentication enforced for all corporate and customer-facing systems
- Endpoint detection and response (EDR) deployed on 100% of corporate endpoints
- Quarterly penetration testing by independent firm
- Annual company-wide phishing simulation and training

**Incident Response:** The company maintains a tested incident response plan with defined escalation paths, communication protocols, and regulatory notification procedures. tabletop exercises are conducted semi-annually.

**Insurance Coverage:** Cyber liability insurance policy provides $10 million in coverage with a $500,000 deductible.

### 7.3 Three Lines of Defense

Risk management responsibilities are allocated across the three lines of defense model:

**First Line (Business Units):** Risk owners are responsible for identifying, assessing, and managing risks within their areas of responsibility. They implement control activities and monitor effectiveness.

**Second Line (Risk and Compliance Functions):** The Enterprise Risk Management team provides oversight, guidance, and coordination. The Compliance function monitors regulatory requirements and coordinates remediation. Internal Legal counsel advises on contractual and regulatory matters.

**Third Line (Internal Audit):** Internal Audit provides independent assurance on the design and effectiveness of the risk management framework and internal controls. Internal Audit reports functionally to the Audit Committee and administratively to the Chief Executive Officer.

## 8. Risk Response Strategies

For each identified risk, one or more response strategies are employed:

**Avoid:** Eliminate the risk entirely by ceasing the associated activity.  
**Reduce:** Implement controls or mitigation measures to reduce likelihood, impact, or both.  
**Share:** Transfer or share risk through insurance, contractual arrangements, or partnerships.  
**Accept:** Acknowledge risk where cost of mitigation exceeds benefit, with appropriate governance approval.  

No risks in the current register are in "Avoid" status. Six risks are in "Share" status (primarily via insurance). Fourteen risks are in "Reduce" status with active mitigation controls. Four risks have been formally accepted by the Board Risk Committee with documented rationale.
